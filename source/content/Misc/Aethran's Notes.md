# Note 1
Day 13 of Firstgrowth, 1299 A.F.

We made camp within the outer passage just before nightfall. Progress has been slower than I would like. The lower tunnels are unstable, and several of the hired hands have begun to voice their concerns more openly.

Morale is… fragile.

I cannot blame them entirely. This place is wrong in ways I struggle to properly articulate. The air is still, yet carries a weight to it, as though the mountain itself is holding its breath. Even the light behaves strangely. Lanterns dim without cause, and shadows linger longer than they should.

Still, we press on.

I have assembled capable people. Scholars, labourers, a pair of sellswords to keep order if needed. They look to me for direction, though I have begun to notice how quickly their eyes turn to the dark between the tunnels.

I must be worthy of that trust.

I find my thoughts returning to her more often in the quiet moments. To them both. I had thought that time might dull the edge of it, but it has not. If anything, the silence only sharpens it.

I do not delude myself. I know what is lost.

But I also know what we have already proven, that even the divine may be bound, shaped, and made to yield.

If Godsbane can anchor such power… then perhaps it can anchor something else.

Something that was taken from me.

Tomorrow, we descend further.

A.V.
# Note 2
Day 14 of Firstgrowth, 1299 A.F.

We have descended beyond the outer passages. The stone here changes. Smoother in places, almost polished, though no tool marks can be found. In others, it twists into shapes that resemble structure without obeying any true design.

We discovered deposits of a dark metal embedded within the walls. It resists heat, resists arcane influence, far more so than any material I have encountered before. When struck, it produces no true ring, only a dull, swallowed tone.

This is no ordinary ore.

If my suspicions are correct, this may be a base component of Godsbane. The “void-iron” referenced in earlier texts. Not merely resistant, but suppressive. As though it does not endure force, but denies it entirely.

I have begun to suspect this place was never meant to be found.

The expedition grows restless.

Harrow refused to continue this morning. Claimed he could hear something beneath the stone. Breathing, he said. A low, rhythmic sound that did not belong to any living thing. I dismissed it as nerves, but he would not be moved. Two others sided with him.

I had the sellswords escort them back to the upper passage.

I do not expect they will wait long before abandoning the expedition entirely.

Let them.

I cannot afford doubt, not now.

We are too close.

The inscriptions here do not speak of gods as creators.

They speak of them as architects. Builders.

Not beings who always were, but those who became.

There is repetition in the phrasing, as though the same truth is being expressed in different forms. I have translated one fragment with some confidence:

_They were named, and so they were known. They were known, and so they were bound._

Bound not in chains, but in definition. In role. In memory.

I cannot shake the sense that what is written here is not reverence, but record. Not worship, but instruction.

If this is true, then what we call divine may simply be what has been remembered… and agreed upon.

I do not yet understand why such a thing would be necessary.

But I am certain now that it was deliberate.

I find myself thinking of her voice. Not as it was at the end, but before. Softer. Steadier.

It is a dangerous thing, memory.

But it is all I have left.

Tomorrow, we press deeper. With or without the others.

A.V.
# Note 3
Day 20 of Firstgrowth, 1299 A.F.

The tunnels no longer follow any sensible geometry. Distance folds in on itself. We walk for hours and arrive somewhere we should not, yet it always feels as though we are being drawn in the right direction.

I have stopped trying to map it. The mountain decides the path, not us.

Another member of the expedition is missing. No sign of struggle. No tracks. Just absence.

The others argue over how many of us remain. I have taken to writing names down at the start and end of each day. Even that feels insufficient.

The sound is constant now. Low and steady, felt through the soles of my boots before it is heard. It carries through the stone like a pulse.

We are approaching something central. I am certain of it.

The void-iron grows thicker the deeper we descend. What began as veins now runs in broad seams through the rock, converging as though drawn toward a single point. It gives the impression of structure rather than deposit.

Last night, I saw them.

I had been alone, reviewing the inscriptions by lantern light, when I heard my name spoken from behind me. I turned, and they were standing there.

My wife.

My daughter.

As they had been, before. Untouched by what came after.

They did not move. They only watched me. There was no fear in them. No accusation. Only… presence.

I remember stepping forward. I remember reaching out.

The light faltered, and they were gone.

The others say I spoke to empty air. One of the sellswords tried to wake me, though I was already standing. I recall none of it clearly.

I have spent the day considering what I saw.

This place interacts with thought. With memory. It draws something out and gives it form, however briefly. That much I cannot deny.

The question is whether that is all it does.

The inscriptions describe Godsbane as an anchor. Something that fixes what would otherwise drift, that holds fast what cannot be held.

If such a thing can bind forces of that scale…

Then there is no reason it could not be applied more precisely.

The others grow more fearful with each passing hour. I can see it in the way they avoid the deeper passages, in how they hesitate when the sound grows louder.

I no longer share that hesitation.

Whatever lies ahead, it is not merely a ruin. It is a mechanism.

And I intend to reach its heart.

A.V.
# Note 4
Day 23 of Firstgrowth, 1299 A.F.

I have reached the lowest chamber.

The descent ended not in a grand structure, but in something far more deliberate. A small, hollowed space beneath the stone, its walls covered in inscription so dense it leaves little bare surface.

At its centre rests a single object.

A fragment of Godsbane.

Dark, unyielding, threaded with veins of silver that catch what little light reaches this depth. It hums faintly when held, not with sound, but with a pressure I feel behind the eyes.

This is what they hid. Not the material itself, but this place. This knowledge.

I have spent what time I can studying both the relic and the surrounding inscriptions. They are clearer here than anywhere above. Intentional. Preserved.

This was meant to be understood.

Godsbane is not a tool of restoration. It does not return what is lost. It fixes, it anchors, it binds. Nothing more.

I tested it.

The result was… conclusive.

What this place shows me cannot be made real. Not with this. Not with anything built for containment.

I will not write more of that. There is no value in it.

The inscriptions speak of something beneath this place. Not here, but deeper still. Not in distance, but in truth.

A name is repeated throughout.

Val’kareth.

It is described not as a god, nor as a force of nature, but as something that exists outside both. Something that was bound, not destroyed.

The ones we call gods are named here as well. Not as creators, but as those who took part in the binding. Mortals, elevated through the telling of it, their true nature lost to time by design.

Elia is named among them.

Not as divine, but as the one who enacted what the inscriptions call the Veil. A deliberate unmaking of memory. The truth of Val’kareth, of the prison, of those who built it, all removed and replaced with something… safer.

A kindness, it says.

A mercy.

I see now what was done, and why.

I also see what it cost.

We have been left with a world that does not understand itself. Gods that are not gods. History that is not history. Truth buried so deeply that it might as well not exist at all.

I came here seeking a way to undo what was taken from me. That path does not lie in this relic.

But it may lie in what was done to the world itself.

The Veil reshaped memory. It defined what is known and what is forgotten. It turned mortals into gods, and truth into myth.

If such a thing can be created…

Then it can be broken.

I do not know what will follow if it falls. The inscriptions offer no guidance there. Perhaps there is none to give.

But I know this.

So long as it remains, the truth is beyond reach.

And so is she.

I will not accept that.

Whatever Elia intended, whatever mercy she believed she was granting…

I will undo it.

A.V.

# Note 5
I see it clearly.

The Veil is not some passive shroud. It is held in place. Maintained. Fed. Not by force, but by structure. By the roles they created.

By them.

The ones we call gods.

They are not beyond it. They are part of it. The anchors that keep it stable. The story holds because they still exist to uphold it. To define it. To be remembered as it requires.

Remove one…

and the structure falters.

It must.

The inscriptions never say it outright, but the pattern is there. The binding below, the rewriting above. One imprisons what cannot be destroyed. The other ensures no one remembers why.

Two halves of the same design.

If one weakens, the other follows.

I had thought the Veil a barrier. Something to be peeled back, unraveled piece by piece.

That was wrong.

It is supported.

And supports can be broken.

Not all of them. That would be madness. Collapse. I am not blind to that.

But one…

One would be enough.

Enough to fracture it. Enough to let the truth bleed through. Enough to reach what lies beneath the lie.

They are not eternal. I have seen proof of that. They can be bound. They can be altered.

They can be killed.

And if one falls…

what happens then?

The Veil weakens. Memory returns. The truth surfaces. The world sees what it has forgotten.

And with that…

so does it.

I understand the risk. I understand it better than anyone.

Val’kareth is not contained by ignorance alone. The Veil was a mercy, yes, but it was also a shield. Remove it, and something ancient will stir. Something vast.

But it is already stirring. The cracks are already there. The world rots at the edges and calls it sickness.

This is not prevention.

This is delay.

And delay has cost me everything.

I will not accept a world built on a lie, sustained by sacrifice, that demands we forget what was taken from us.

If the price of truth is the death of a god, then so be it.

If the cost is worse…

then let it come.

I will break the Veil.
