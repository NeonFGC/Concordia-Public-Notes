My dearest Aethran,

Your last letter reached me beneath the silver moonlight of Eryndor, and I confess I have read it no fewer than five times since. Each reading brings the same warmth to my heart. To know that you and Lysa have been granted such a blessing… it is a light I had feared long absent from our age.

I offer you both my sincerest congratulations. May the child inherit your curiosity, and her strength.

Perhaps one day I too shall be so fortunate. For now, however, my attention remains, as ever, fixed upon the work we began at the Academy. To that end, I have some exciting news.

The expedition beneath Lake Silvermere proved fruitful. Deep beneath its calm surface we discovered a small shrine, and within it, a fragment of the material we once only dared to theorise about. At last, our work has borne fruit. The inscriptions upon the ruin name it Godsbane.

I have arranged for the fragment to be transferred to the Aetherium Vault, where it will remain secure until we can properly study it.

More curious still, the inscriptions seem to reference Helgrind. Indirectly, yes, but clearly enough that I am certain more of the material lies there. If your earlier translations hold true, it may be one of the oldest sites in all Concordia.

But that is a matter for another time.

For now, you have far greater concerns than ancient alloys and buried ruins.

Take care of your family, my friend, and write soon. I would hear everything.

With love,  
Caelthir Vaeloras
