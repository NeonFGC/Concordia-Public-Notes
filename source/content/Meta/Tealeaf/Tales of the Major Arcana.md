### 0. The Fool
The target can immediately use its reaction to move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks. Until the end of its next turn, it has advantage on one ability check or saving throw of its choice.

---
### I. The Magician

You make a ranged spell attack against a creature you can see within 60 feet. On a hit, the target takes force damage equal to **two rolls of your Bardic Inspiration die + your Charisma modifier**, and you gain advantage on the next spell attack you make before the end of your next turn.

---
### II. The High Priestess

For 10 minutes, the target gains advantage on Wisdom (Perception) and Wisdom (Insight) checks. Once during this duration, the target can roll an extra **Bardic Inspiration die** and add it to a Wisdom saving throw.

---
### III. The Empress

The target and one creature of its choice it can see within 30 feet each gain temporary hit points equal to **a roll of your Bardic Inspiration die + your Charisma modifier**. While these temporary hit points last, the creatures have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened.

---
### IV. The Emperor

For 1 minute, the target gains a +2 bonus to AC, and creatures of the target’s choice within 10 feet of it have advantage on saving throws against being frightened.

---
### V. The Hierophant

The target regains hit points equal to **two rolls of your Bardic Inspiration die + your Charisma modifier**, and one effect causing the target to be charmed, frightened, or poisoned ends.

---
### VI. The Lovers

For 1 minute, whenever the target is within 10 feet of a creature it considers an ally, both creatures gain a +1 bonus to attack rolls and saving throws. This bonus ends if they are separated by more than 10 feet.

---
### VII. The Chariot

The target can immediately use its reaction to move up to its speed. Until the end of its next turn, the target has advantage on Strength checks, Dexterity checks, and Strength or Dexterity saving throws.

---
### VIII. Strength

For 1 minute, the target’s weapon attacks deal extra damage equal to **a roll of your Bardic Inspiration die**, and the target has advantage on Strength (Athletics) checks.

---
### IX. The Hermit

The target becomes invisible until the end of its next turn or until it makes an attack or casts a spell. When the invisibility ends, the target gains temporary hit points equal to **a roll of your Bardic Inspiration die**.

---
### X. Wheel of Fortune

Roll your Bardic Inspiration die. For the next minute, the target can add the number rolled to **one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw** of its choice, and subtract the same number from the next attack roll made against it.

---
### XI. Justice

You force a creature you can see within 60 feet to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, it takes psychic damage equal to **two rolls of your Bardic Inspiration die**, and the next attack it makes before the end of its next turn has disadvantage.

---
### XII. The Hanged Man

The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be **restrained** until the end of its next turn. While restrained this way, it also takes psychic damage equal to **a roll of your Bardic Inspiration die**.

---
### XIII. Death

You make a melee or ranged spell attack against a creature. On a hit, the target takes necrotic damage equal to **three rolls of your Bardic Inspiration die**. If this damage reduces the creature to 0 hit points, you regain one expended use of Bardic Inspiration.

---
### XIV. Temperance

For 1 minute, the target gains resistance to one damage type of your choice (acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder), chosen when the tale is invoked.

---
### XV. The Devil

The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be **charmed** by you until the end of its next turn. While charmed this way, it takes psychic damage equal to **a roll of your Bardic Inspiration die** at the start of its turn.

---
### XVI. The Tower

Each creature of your choice within a 10-foot-radius sphere centered on a point you can see within 60 feet must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a failure, a creature takes thunder damage equal to **three rolls of your Bardic Inspiration die** and is knocked prone; on a success, it takes half damage and isn’t knocked prone.

---
### XVII. The Star

The target regains hit points equal to **a roll of your Bardic Inspiration die + your bard level**, and gains advantage on its next saving throw made within the next minute.

---
### XVIII. The Moon

For 1 minute, dim light within 20 feet of the target becomes lightly obscured. Enemies of the target in that area have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks, and the target has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks.

---
### XIX. The Sun

Each creature of the target’s choice within 10 feet of it gains temporary hit points equal to **a roll of your Bardic Inspiration die**, and the area sheds bright light in a 20-foot radius for 1 minute. While in this light, allies have advantage on saving throws against being frightened.

---
### XX. Judgement

Each creature of your choice within 30 feet that can hear you must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, a creature takes psychic damage equal to **two rolls of your Bardic Inspiration die** and is **stunned until the end of its next turn**. On a success, it takes half damage and isn’t stunned.

---
### XXI. The World

For 1 minute, the target gains a +1 bonus to AC, attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws. At the end of the duration, the target gains temporary hit points equal to **a roll of your Bardic Inspiration die**.
